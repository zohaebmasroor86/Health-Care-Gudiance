Boiled potatoes, for example, have a GI of 78. The GI scores for some popular vegetables are: Frozen green peas score 39 on the GI index. Carrots score 41 when boiled and 16 when raw. Low-GI vegetables are also safe for people with diabetes, such as: artichoke, asparagus, broccoli, green beans, lettuce, peppers, spinach, celery It is important to note that the GI gives a relative value to each food item and does not refer to the specific sugar content. Glycemic load (GL) refers to how much glucose will enter the body in one serving of a food.
